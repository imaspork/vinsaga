AutoPair for iOS

Privacy Policy

We built AutoPair to be simple and private. Here's exactly how we handle your data.

Effective June 1, 2025
The short version

AutoPair collects no personal data. The app manages Bluetooth connections on your device and nothing is ever stored, transmitted, or shared. What happens on your device, stays on your device.

What we collect

AutoPair does not collect, store, or transmit any personal information. The app operates entirely on your device to manage Bluetooth device connections.

No personal data No analytics No crash reports No advertising IDs Bluetooth access (on-device only)

Bluetooth access

AutoPair requires access to your device's Bluetooth functionality in order to discover and manage device pairings. This access is used solely to perform the core function of the app. No Bluetooth data, device names, or pairing history is stored or sent anywhere.

Third-party services

AutoPair does not use any third-party analytics, advertising, or crash-reporting services. There are no SDKs or frameworks embedded in the app that collect data.

Data retention

Because we collect no data, there is nothing to retain or delete. All Bluetooth operations are handled in real time on your device and nothing is persisted beyond your active app session.

Children's privacy

AutoPair does not collect any information from anyone, including children under the age of 13. The app is safe for use by all ages.

Changes to this policy

If we ever update this privacy policy, the new version will be posted at this URL with a revised effective date. We encourage you to review this page periodically. Any future changes will not be retroactive.
